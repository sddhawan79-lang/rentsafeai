5Tenant & Third-Party Data

⚖ Important — Controller / Processor Split

This section defines the legal responsibility for tenant personal data. Please read it carefully.

5.1 You Are the Data Controller for Tenant Data

When you upload, enter, or otherwise submit personal data about your tenants or other third parties (including names, contact details, identification documents, financial records, or any other personal information) to the Service, you act as the data controller for that data. You determine the purposes and means of processing that personal data in your capacity as a landlord or property manager.

We act as your data processor in respect of tenant data, processing it solely on your documented instructions to provide the Service. This relationship is governed by the data processing provisions in our Terms & Conditions (clause 12) and our Data Processing Agreement.

5.2 Your Obligations as Controller

As data controller for tenant data, you are solely responsible for:

  • Ensuring you have a lawful basis under UK GDPR for processing each category of tenant personal data you upload;
  • Providing tenants with appropriate privacy notices explaining how their data will be processed, including that it may be processed using AI-assisted tools and stored with cloud service providers;
  • Ensuring that any special category data (including copies of identification documents, health-related information, or similar) is processed with appropriate additional safeguards and a valid Article 9 condition;
  • Responding to any data subject access requests, erasure requests, or other rights exercises from your tenants in your capacity as controller;
  • Ensuring your use of the Service does not cause us to breach applicable data protection law.

5.3 Our Obligations as Processor

As your data processor, we will:

  • Process tenant data only on your documented instructions and only to the extent necessary to provide the Service;
  • Implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures to protect tenant data;
  • Not engage sub-processors for tenant data processing without ensuring equivalent protections apply (see section 8);
  • Notify you without undue delay if we become aware of a personal data breach affecting tenant data;
  • Assist you, so far as reasonably practicable, in responding to data subject rights requests relating to tenant data we hold on your behalf;
  • Delete or return tenant data to you upon termination of your account, in accordance with our data retention policy and your instructions.

5.4 No Liability for Unlawful Uploads

We accept no liability whatsoever for any processing of personal data that you carry out in your capacity as data controller, including any failure by you to: obtain a lawful basis; provide adequate privacy notices to tenants; handle special category data lawfully; or comply with any data subject rights request. Any regulatory investigation, fine, or enforcement action arising from your conduct as data controller is entirely your responsibility.

6.3 Minimisation

9International Data Transfers

The United Kingdom currently recognises certain countries as providing adequate protection for personal data. The United States is not currently a UK-recognised adequate country for general data transfers. Where personal data is transferred to our US-based sub-processors (Anthropic and Resend), we rely on the following safeguards:

9.1 Standard Contractual Clauses

We have entered into, or require our US sub-processors to operate under, the International Data Transfer Agreements (IDTAs) approved by the ICO, or the equivalent EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) with appropriate UK addendums, as the lawful transfer mechanism for personal data transferred to the United States. These contractual safeguards require our sub-processors to protect personal data to a standard equivalent to UK GDPR.

9.2 AI Processing Transfer (Anthropic)

When you use AI-assisted features, the data submitted for processing is transferred to Anthropic's servers in the United States. This transfer is covered by Standard Contractual Clauses as described above. Anthropic processes data solely for the purpose of returning AI-generated outputs and does not use your data to train its models under its API terms. You should nevertheless avoid submitting unnecessary personal data in AI processing requests and should not submit special category data (such as copies of passports or health information) unless strictly necessary for the feature in question.

9.3 Email Delivery Transfer (Resend)

Transactional emails (including compliance reminders, onboarding emails, and account notifications) are delivered via Resend's infrastructure, which operates from the United States. Email delivery involves the processing of recipient email addresses and the content of the email. This transfer is covered by Standard Contractual Clauses.

9.4 Supabase

Your primary account and property data is stored by Supabase on AWS infrastructure located in the EU West region (eu-west-2, London). This data does not leave the UK/EU and no international transfer mechanism is required for Supabase data storage. Supabase operates under a Data Processing Agreement that is compliant with UK GDPR.

11Your Rights

Under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data. These rights apply to data for which we are the data controller (i.e. your account and usage data). For tenant data, rights must be exercised with you in your capacity as data controller.

Right of Access (Art. 15)
You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you and information about how we process it. We will respond within one calendar month.
Right to Rectification (Art. 16)
You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you. Much of your account data can be corrected directly within the Service.
Right to Erasure (Art. 17)
You have the right to request deletion of your personal data where: it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected; you withdraw consent (where consent was the basis); or we have no legitimate grounds to retain it. This right is subject to our legal obligations to retain certain data.
Right to Restrict Processing (Art. 18)
You have the right to request that we restrict our processing of your personal data in certain circumstances, such as where you contest its accuracy or object to our processing.
Right to Data Portability (Art. 20)
Where processing is based on contract or consent and carried out by automated means, you have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format and to transmit it to another controller.
Right to Object (Art. 21)
You have the right to object to processing based on our legitimate interests. We will cease such processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, or the processing is necessary for legal claims.
Rights in Relation to Automated Decision-Making (Art. 22)
You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing that produces legal or significant effects. We do not make such automated decisions — see section 6.2.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at support@rentsafeai.co.uk. We will respond within one calendar month. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request. There is no charge for exercising your rights in most circumstances.

Right to complain: If you are dissatisfied with how we handle your personal data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113. We would, however, appreciate the opportunity to address your concerns directly before you contact the ICO.

12Security

We take the security of personal data seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it against unauthorised access, accidental loss, destruction, or disclosure. Our security measures include:

  • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted between your browser and our Service is encrypted using TLS (HTTPS);
  • Encryption at rest: Data stored in our Supabase database is encrypted at rest using AES-256;
  • Access controls: Access to personal data is restricted on a need-to-know basis using role-based access controls and authenticated sessions;
  • Password security: User passwords are hashed using industry-standard algorithms and are never stored in plaintext;
  • Infrastructure security: We rely on Supabase's enterprise-grade security infrastructure, which includes regular security audits and penetration testing;
  • Document storage: Uploaded documents are stored in private, access-controlled Supabase Storage buckets and are not publicly accessible.

Notwithstanding the above, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security and are not liable for security breaches that are outside our reasonable control, including breaches resulting from your failure to keep your login credentials secure.

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the ICO in accordance with our obligations under UK GDPR (within 72 hours of becoming aware, where feasible).
