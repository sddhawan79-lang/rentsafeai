2Nature of AI-Generated Outputs

All outputs generated by AI features of the Service are produced by large language models that predict likely and useful responses based on the input provided. You must understand the following fundamental characteristics of such outputs:

What AI Can Do

  • Extract structured data from documents
  • Identify potential compliance gaps
  • Draft notices based on templates
  • Summarise tenancy information
  • Flag issues for human review
  • Assist with compliance checklists

What AI Cannot Do

  • Guarantee legal accuracy
  • Replace a solicitor or legal adviser
  • Confirm regulatory compliance
  • Verify document authenticity
  • Account for all individual circumstances
  • Predict court or tribunal outcomes

AI outputs are probabilistic, not deterministic. The same input may produce different outputs on different occasions. Outputs that appear confident, detailed, or professionally formatted are not thereby more reliable — the presentation of an output does not indicate its legal accuracy or completeness.

3What AI Features Do

The AI features within RentSafe AI are designed to assist you with the organisational and administrative aspects of property management. Specifically:

3.1 Document Scanning

AI document scanning extracts structured information (such as tenant names, dates, addresses, and key terms) from documents you upload. Extracted data may be incomplete, misread, or incorrectly interpreted — particularly from handwritten, scanned, low-resolution, or non-standard documents. You must always verify extracted data against the original document before relying on it.

3.2 Compliance Analysis

The compliance health score and related analysis are generated based on data you have entered into the platform. They reflect only what you have told the system — not the actual state of your property or tenancy. A high compliance score does not mean you are legally compliant; it means the data you have entered suggests compliance. Incomplete or inaccurate data input will produce unreliable compliance assessments.

3.3 Notice and Document Drafting

AI-assisted notice drafting (including Section 8 notices, onboarding documents, and similar) produces template-based drafts informed by your input. These drafts are starting points only. They must be reviewed by a qualified solicitor before being served on a tenant or used in any legal proceedings. An incorrectly drafted or served notice may be invalid and could prejudice your legal position.

3.4 AI Assistant

The AI Assistant provides conversational responses to questions about landlord compliance, housing legislation, and property management. Responses are based on training data and contextual information from your portfolio. They do not constitute legal advice, may not reflect the most recent legislative changes, and should not be relied upon as authoritative statements of law.

3.5 Reminders and Alerts

Compliance reminders and deadline alerts are generated based on dates and information you have entered. They are informational tools only and do not confirm that you are meeting your legal obligations. You remain solely responsible for tracking all statutory deadlines independently of the Service.

4Critical Limitations

The following limitations apply to all AI-generated outputs without exception. No AI feature of this platform overrides these limitations regardless of how the output is presented.

4.1 May Contain Errors

AI outputs may contain factual errors, legal inaccuracies, omissions, hallucinations (plausible-sounding but incorrect information), or outdated content. We do not warrant the accuracy, completeness, or currency of any AI-generated output.

4.2 Not Updated in Real Time

The AI model used by the Service has a training data cutoff date and may not reflect the most recent changes to housing legislation, case law, regulatory guidance, or government policy. The Renters' Rights Act 2024, associated regulations, and related guidance are subject to ongoing development — AI outputs relating to these matters may not reflect the latest position.

4.3 Does Not Know Your Full Circumstances

AI outputs are based only on the data you have entered into the platform. The AI has no knowledge of facts, circumstances, or legal issues that you have not disclosed. Complex tenancy situations, disputes, pre-existing legal proceedings, vulnerable tenants, or unusual property circumstances require advice from a qualified solicitor who has full knowledge of your position.

4.4 Specific Exclusions

The AI features of this Service do not and cannot:

  • Confirm the legal validity of a Gas Safety Certificate (CP12), Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR), Energy Performance Certificate (EPC), or any other statutory certificate or document;
  • Verify that a deposit has been correctly protected or that prescribed information has been validly served;
  • Confirm that a Right to Rent check has been lawfully carried out;
  • Confirm that a Section 8 notice has been correctly drafted, addressed, or served;
  • Confirm that any document meets the requirements of the Deregulation Act 2015, the Renters' Rights Act 2024, or any other applicable legislation;
  • Provide advice on individual legal disputes, court proceedings, or tribunal applications;
  • Replace inspection of the physical property by a qualified professional.
Important: Reliance on AI outputs without independent verification — particularly in relation to Section 8 notices, deposit compliance, and statutory certificates — could result in invalid notices, financial penalties, or adverse possession proceedings. Always seek qualified legal advice before taking formal action against a tenant.

7Document Processing Warnings

The following specific warnings apply each time you use AI document processing features:

  • Extraction accuracy: Text extracted from PDFs, images, or scanned documents may contain errors introduced by OCR or AI interpretation. Always compare extracted data to the original document;
  • Handwritten documents: AI accuracy for handwritten content is significantly lower than for typed text. Do not rely on AI extraction of handwritten documents without thorough manual review;
  • Multi-party documents: Documents involving multiple tenants, guarantors, or complex tenancy structures may not be correctly interpreted by AI. Review all party details manually;
  • Dates and deadlines: AI-extracted dates — including tenancy start dates, notice periods, and certificate expiry dates — must be verified against original documents. An incorrect date could invalidate a notice or cause a missed statutory deadline;
  • Financial figures: Rent amounts, deposit sums, and other financial data extracted by AI must be verified against original documents and your own records before being used for any purpose.

9Section 8 Notices & Legal Documents

The Section 8 notice drafting feature and all other legal document generation features carry the following specific warnings:

  • Notices generated by the Service are drafts only and must be reviewed by a qualified solicitor before service;
  • The correct ground(s) for possession must be identified by you and/or your legal adviser — the AI may suggest grounds based on your input, but this suggestion does not constitute legal advice and may be incorrect;
  • Notice periods, prescribed forms, and service requirements under the Renters' Rights Act 2024 and predecessor legislation are subject to change — you must verify that the notice meets current statutory requirements before serving it;
  • An incorrectly drafted, incorrectly addressed, or incorrectly served Section 8 notice is likely to be invalid and will not found a valid possession claim;
  • Serving an invalid notice may delay possession proceedings, expose you to wasted costs, or — in certain circumstances involving harassment or unlawful eviction — result in criminal liability;
  • The Service does not provide advice on service of notices. You are responsible for ensuring valid service in accordance with the tenancy agreement and applicable law.
Do not serve any AI-generated legal document on a tenant without first having it reviewed by a qualified solicitor. The potential consequences of serving an invalid notice — including failed possession proceedings, wasted legal costs, and counterclaims — significantly outweigh the cost of professional review.
