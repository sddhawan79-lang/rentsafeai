Complaints Policy

Effective from 10 May 2026 Version 1.0
We take complaints seriously. This policy explains how to raise a concern about the NexLet AI platform, our service, or your data. We aim to resolve all complaints fairly, promptly, and in accordance with UK consumer protection law.

01 Our commitment

NexLet AI is operated by NexLet AI Limited ("we", "us", "our"). We are committed to providing a reliable, compliant platform for UK private landlords. If something goes wrong, we want to hear about it so we can put it right.

We will:

  • Treat every complaint fairly, seriously, and without bias.
  • Acknowledge your complaint within 2 working days of receipt.
  • Investigate thoroughly and respond with a resolution or next steps within 10 working days.
  • Keep you informed if a complaint requires more time to investigate, explaining the reason and expected timeline.
  • Maintain a record of all complaints for internal review and service improvement.

02 What you can complain about

You can raise a complaint about any aspect of the NexLet AI service, including:

  • Platform functionality — bugs, errors, or unexpected behaviour in the dashboard, document generation, or tenant portal.
  • Billing and payments — incorrect charges, failed payments, cancellation requests, or refund enquiries.
  • Data and privacy — concerns about how your personal data or your tenants' data is handled, stored, or shared.
  • AI-generated content — inaccurate, incomplete, or inappropriate output from our AI document generator or chat assistant.
  • Account access — login issues, suspended accounts, or unauthorised access concerns.
  • Email communications — problems with automated alerts, missing notifications, or spam concerns.
  • General service quality — any other aspect of the platform or service that has fallen below your expectations.
Important note for tenants: If you are a tenant and have a complaint about your landlord (for example, about repairs, deposits, or the property condition), NexLet AI is the wrong contact. You should raise your concern directly with your landlord, or contact Citizens Advice, Shelter, or your local council's private renting team.

03 How to make a complaint

By email (preferred)

Email: complaints@nexlet.co.uk
Subject line: Please include "Complaint" in the subject and briefly describe the issue.

When submitting a complaint, please include:

  • Your full name and the email address associated with your NexLet AI account.
  • A clear description of the issue, including dates and any relevant details.
  • What you believe would be a fair resolution.
  • Any supporting evidence (screenshots, error messages, email headers, billing receipts).

We may ask for additional information to help us investigate. The more detail you provide upfront, the faster we can assist.

04 Our complaints process

Stage 1 — Acknowledgement: We will confirm receipt of your complaint within 2 working days and assign a reference number.

Stage 2 — Investigation: Your complaint will be reviewed by a member of our customer support team. We may contact you for further information. We aim to complete our investigation within 10 working days.

Stage 3 — Response: We will send you a written response explaining:

  • Our findings and the outcome of our investigation.
  • Any action we have taken or will take to resolve the issue.
  • If applicable, an explanation of why a different outcome was reached than you requested.
  • Your right to escalate if you remain dissatisfied.

Stage 4 — Escalation: If you are not satisfied with our Stage 3 response, you may request that your complaint be reviewed by a senior member of management. We will respond to escalated complaints within a further 10 working days.

05 Complaints about data protection

If your complaint relates to how we handle your personal data, you have the right to complain directly to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO). We would appreciate the opportunity to resolve your concern first, but you are not required to contact us before approaching the ICO.

Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)

Website: ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint
Helpline: 0303 123 1113
Address: Information Commissioner's Office, Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire SK9 5AF

06 Alternative Dispute Resolution

If we are unable to resolve your complaint and you are a consumer (not acting in the course of business), you may be entitled to refer your complaint to an approved Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) provider. NexLet AI does not currently subscribe to a specific ADR scheme, but we remain open to resolving disputes through informal negotiation and will always act in good faith to reach a fair outcome.

For disputes relating to our services, the courts of England and Wales shall have exclusive jurisdiction.

07 Our promise to you

  • You will always be treated with courtesy and respect.
  • Your complaint will never affect your right to continue using our service.
  • We will learn from every complaint and use feedback to improve the platform.
  • We keep records of all complaints for 24 months after closure.
